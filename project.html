The impact of COVID19 Media Hype on the cryptocurrency market

Project aims

The project aims to examine how COVID19-Media Hype affected the cryptocurrency market. First, I aim to find when the effect started appearing by looking at the evolution of the COVID19 Media Hype Index and the two most prominent cryptocurrencies: Bitcoin and Ethereum. Then, I examine Bitcoin’s importance to identify how the market behaves, before establishing the relationship between the price of Bitcoin and COVID-19-Media Hype. Lastly, I examine how cryptocurrency market sentiment changed as COVID-19-Media Hype increased, using data on the Cryptocurrency Fear&Greed (FNG) Index.

Looking at the evolution of Bitcoin and Ethereum, in early 2020, both experience a reduction in their price, which seems to begin in March. Bitcoin's effect on the market

Firstly, it is important to understand that Bitcoin has the final word when it comes to cryptocurrency market sentiment. As shown on the chart below, Bitcoin and Etherium combined, usually represent approximately 60% of the cryptocurrency market dominance. Compared to the market dominance of the rest cryptocurrencies, it is evident that BTC and ETH have a strong effect on the whole market. However, Bitcoin's market dominance is still twice the one of Ethereum's. Regressing BTC/USD on ETH/USD, from the start of the pandemic until now, yields an r-squared value of 0.82. In this model, this indicates that 82% of the variation in Ethereum's price is explained by the price of Bitcoin, showing how much changes in Bitcoin's price can affect the market.

COVID19 declared a pandemic

Having established Bitcoin’s importance, it is useful to look at what happened to it in March 2020, when the WorldHealthOrganisation declared COVID-19 a pandemic. Being declared a pandemic brought COVID-19 a lot of media attention, with the media-hype index increasing from 28.57 on March 10 to 69.2 by the end of the month. The price dip on March 11 is evident on the candlestick chart. Bitcoin's highest price of $8043 on March 10 fell to a price low of $4631 on March 12; a decrease of 42%. However, can this price fall be attributed to COVID-19 Media Hype?

The regression chart below shows the relationship between BTC/USD and COVID-19 MHI by month, from Jan2020 to Dec2021. A regression of COVID-19 MHI on BTC/USD in March 2020 yields an r-squared of 0.59, meaning 59% of the variation in BTC/USD in March 2020 can be explained by the MHI. In this model, this is sufficient to indicate a moderate negative correlation: as COVID-19-media hype increased, BTC/USD decreased. Did that affect the whole market?

The Crypto Fear and Greed (FNG) Index

To look at this, I charted the Crypto FNG together with the COVID-19 MHI. The lower the FNG Index value, the higher the “fear” about investing in cryptocurrencies is in the market. Again, there seems to be a large fall in the FNG value in March 2020 that persists until the end of the month, showing that there was extreme fear in the market when COVID19-media hype started peaking.

Seemingly, evidence of COVID19-media hype affecting the cryptocurrency market is stronger at early stages of the pandemic. As it can be seen on the FNG chart, when COVID19-media hype started falling, market sentiment improved. In some sense, this is because people eventually started adapting to COVID-19. This is proven by the interactive BTC/USD vs MHI regression chart, which shows that as the pandemic progressed, the r-squared value started decreasing. However, cryptocurrencies are extremely volatile and are affected by many factors, such as other media influences as well as market regulations. Therefore, further work that would combine such factors would provide more evidence of how COVID-19 affected the market as the pandemic progressed.

Data used and how I accessed it

  • Cryptocurrency prices: from FRED using Colab(notebook), investing.com and through API using coinapi.com.
  • COVID19-media hype: through CSV files provided byRavenPackTeam.
  • Cryptocurrency market dominance: using an API from Nomics so that the data updates automatically
  • FNG data: accessed through Alternative API but worked more reliably when stored as CSV.

    • Challenges in data cleaning and/or analysis

    The biggest challenge was finding COVID19-media hype data, managing to access it and cleaning it. Time was spent searching for media hype data and to access the data I contacted RavenPack who sent me CSV files that had to be cleaned using Excel as they included unnecessary data. Other challenges include cleaning cryptocurrency price data that included commas. This interfered with analysis, therefore data-cleaning was done using GoogleColab(Notebook). Joining COVID-19 MHI with cryptocurrency FNG and price data also proved a challenge. Different files had different time formats and they had to be manually edited in Excel before data-joins were performed. The files for interactive charts had to be edited to include "year/month" values to work with Vega-lite transformations. Data analysis was simpler except from challenges in creating the candlestick chart that were solved using the Vega-Lite documentation and examples.
